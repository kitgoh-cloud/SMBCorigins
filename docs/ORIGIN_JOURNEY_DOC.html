01 · The Vision

Five days, not five months.

Corporate banking onboarding is historically the most painful phase of a relationship. A Japanese MNC opening accounts across Singapore, Hong Kong, and the UK waits 3–6 months, submits the same documents five times to five different teams, and chases their RM weekly. We can do this in days.

The premise

SMBC has already signalled where it's heading. In January 2026 SMBC Americas rolled out Fenergo for client lifecycle management — a multi-year commitment to simplifying onboarding infrastructure. In April 2026 it launched SMBC Connect, the new transaction banking portal, stating that "cash management systems should be as intuitive and frictionless as the apps on our smartphones."

SMBC Origin is the missing front door — the onboarding experience that feeds directly into SMBC Connect. It's a future-state prototype showing what becomes possible when agentic AI, radical UX clarity, and embedded intelligence are applied end-to-end to the corporate onboarding journey.

"Today, a Japanese MNC opening accounts across SG, HK, and the UK waits three months. With SMBC Origin, the RM sends one invite. AI unwraps the structure, extracts data from every document, screens every entity, configures products across every jurisdiction, and drafts the credit memo. The client is live in days — and their RM spent their time on the relationship, not paperwork."
— The 30-second pitch

Design principles

  • AI is quiet, not loud. Fresh Green is rationed — when you see it, intelligence happened. The cockpit is calm; the AI is a well-dressed colleague, not a chatbot.
  • Parallel, not sequential. The RM and client operate in parallel lanes. AI bridges them. Nothing blocks waiting for a human to push a button unless judgment is genuinely required.
  • Explain the magic. Every AI decision is auditable. Confidence scores, source registries, extraction logs. The RM is in command, not overruled.
  • Heritage through restraint. Japanese design honours — IBM wouldn't call them this, but whitespace, precision, and considered typography. Kanji as watermark, not decoration.
  • Demo every moment. This is a showpiece. If an AI moment isn't visually memorable, it fails the mission.

05 · Stage 2

Entity & Structure

STAGE 02 · DAY 1

AI unwraps the corporate tree.

Expected duration: 4 hours · Hero moment · UBO Intelligence Agent

Historically this stage eats 3–4 weeks. Clients list subsidiaries manually, compliance teams dig through registries one jurisdiction at a time, and UBOs are often identified by self-attestation. We replace all of that with an agent that recursively queries registries across jurisdictions and unwraps shell layers until natural persons are identified.

What happens

Client · Yuki
  • Opens /client/entity
  • Reviews AI-proposed entity tree (pre-built from public registries)
  • Confirms or corrects subsidiary list
  • Identifies UBOs (with AI suggestions)
  • Submits for structure approval
RM · James
  • Monitors AI progress from cockpit
  • Reviews final structure
  • Approves structure (signs off AI's work)
  • Escalates any shell / complex holdings to compliance if needed
AI · UBO Intelligence Agent
  • Queries ACRA (SG), Companies House (UK), Companies Registry (HK), hōjin bangō (JP)
  • Builds entity tree recursively — layer by layer
  • Unwraps shell companies (BVI, Cayman, etc.) until natural persons identified
  • Flags confidence per node (94% = confirmed via registry; 68% = inferred)
  • Produces narrative: "I found 4 subsidiaries, 1 BVI shell, 7 ultimate natural persons"
◆ Hero Moment · UBO Intelligence Agent

The tree that builds itself.

The signature demo moment. When James clicks "Run structure analysis" the UI animates the tree building itself, layer by layer, with live narration. Each node pulses in as it resolves. Shell companies appear as dashed nodes. The natural persons appear last, at the top of the tree, with confidence scores.

Storyboard
  1. "Querying Japan corporate registry for Kaisei Manufacturing KK..." → Parent node appears at centre
  2. "Found 4 subsidiary entities. Querying ACRA, CR-HK, Companies House..." → 4 child nodes fan out with subtle stagger
  3. "Detected holding company in BVI. Unwrapping..." → shell company appears as dashed outline
  4. "Resolved ownership via BVI Financial Services filings." → shell replaced by its backers (Tanaka Family Trust)
  5. "Resolved 7 natural persons at top of structure. Confidence 94%." → UBOs appear at top in gold
Tech sketch

React Flow for the tree. Framer Motion for node reveals (stagger 120ms). Narration as streaming text beside tree. Mocked registry data returned with realistic delays (800ms per query). On production this would orchestrate real registry APIs + Claude to reason across layers.

The Kaisei structure (seed data)

Key data captured this stage
  • All legal entities with registration numbers, jurisdictions, addresses
  • Ownership percentages + control types
  • UBOs (name, DOB, nationality, ownership %, control type)
  • PEP indicator per UBO (flagged for Stage 4 screening)
  • Confidence score per node (for audit trail)
06 · Stage 3

Documentation

STAGE 03 · DAY 2

Drag in a doc. Watch it unpack itself.

Expected duration: 6 hours · Hero moment · Doc Extraction Agent

Instead of the bank asking for 15 documents from a generic checklist, the AI asks only for what's actually missing per jurisdiction + risk tier. When the client drags in a PDF, fields populate in real time, inconsistencies are flagged against the entity tree, and the RM sees extracted data with source citations.

What happens

Client · Yuki
  • Opens /client/documents
  • Sees smart checklist: "We need 7 documents. We already have 3 from Japan; you uploaded 1 in Stage 2."
  • Drags Certificate of Incorporation for Singapore entity → extraction begins live
  • Reviews extracted fields (editable), confirms
  • Responds to AI query: "This address differs from ACRA record — please clarify"
RM · James
  • Monitors doc queue with real-time completion %
  • Reviews flagged inconsistencies, dispositions
  • Approves doc package for Stage 4
AI · Doc Extraction Agent
  • OCR + LLM reads uploaded PDF
  • Populates structured fields (registration number, directors, share capital, etc.)
  • Cross-references extractions against Stage 2 entity tree
  • Flags discrepancies with confidence + suggestion
  • Translates Japanese-language documents when needed

Smart doc requests (not generic checklists)

Different jurisdictions require different documents. A static checklist dumps the kitchen sink on the client. The smart version asks only for what's actually needed given:

  • The jurisdiction of each entity (SG, HK, UK, JP each have different mandatory docs)
  • What's already been pulled from public registries
  • The client's risk tier (simple structure + low risk = lighter package)
  • Which products were selected (credit needs audited financials; accounts don't)

07 · Stage 4

Screening (KYC / AML)

STAGE 04 · DAY 3

Every entity, every person, every list.

Expected duration: 2 hours · Agentic screening

Runs all entities and UBOs through sanctions lists, PEP databases, adverse media, and internal watchlists. Agentic layer clears low-risk false positives automatically, writes a narrative justification per hit, and escalates genuine amber/red to the RM with context.

What happens

Client · Yuki
  • Sees live status: "We're screening 12 entities and 7 individuals"
  • Gets notified if additional information is required (rare)
  • Otherwise stays hands-off — this is a backend step
RM · James
  • Reviews screening dashboard: clears, ambers, reds
  • Dispositions amber hits with context + rationale
  • Escalates reds to compliance with 1-click handoff
  • Signs off on composite risk rating
AI · Screening Agent
  • Parallel-runs OFAC, UN, EU, HMT sanctions against all subjects
  • Checks PEP registries (commercial + gov)
  • Scans adverse media (last 5 years, weighted)
  • Auto-clears obvious false positives (same-name, wrong DOB/country)
  • Writes narrative per flagged hit: "Hit on X because Y. My read: Z."
  • Composes composite risk score

08 · Stage 5

Products & Credit

STAGE 05 · DAY 4

Configure. Price. Memo. Approve.

Expected duration: 1 day · Hero moment · Credit Memo Drafter

The meatiest stage by far. Every product needs configuration: accounts (currencies, signatories, limits), FX (pairs, forwards), trade lines (LC/SBLC), and the big one — the credit facility (USD 50M revolving). AI proposes optimal structures and drafts the credit memo; RM reviews and approves.

What happens · per product

Accounts Currencies, signatories, daily limits Multi-entity structure, sweep setup Against policy, pricing
Cash Mgmt Payment types, approval rules Notional pooling design Operational fit
FX Pairs, expected volumes Pricing tiers, hedging framework Spread, exposure
Trade LC/SBLC needs, counterparties Line sizing from trade flow data Credit exposure
Credit Facility ask, tenor, purpose Full credit memo drafted Sign-off + pricing
◆ Hero Moment · Credit Memo Drafter

From audited statements to credit memo in minutes.

RM clicks "Draft credit memo". Memo template appears. Each section streams in as AI writes it, with source pins linking back to the data it used. RM reviews section by section — Accept / Edit / Regenerate.

Memo structure
  1. Executive summary — one paragraph, the ask, the recommendation
  2. Client overview — from entity tree + industry data
  3. Financial analysis — 3y spread from audited financials (revenue, EBITDA, debt, coverage)
  4. Proposed facility structure — size, tenor, purpose, drawdown mechanics
  5. Risk rating & key risks — from screening + industry + financial analysis
  6. Covenants & conditions precedent — suggested, pulled from policy library
  7. Recommendation — approve / approve with conditions / decline

09 · Stage 6

Activation

STAGE 06 · DAY 5

Go live on SMBC Connect.

Expected duration: 1 hour · Ceremony + handoff

The closing ceremony. Client receives credentials + welcome pack. Handoff to service team. Relationship dashboard spun up in SMBC Connect. Perpetual KYC monitoring begins in the background.

What happens

Client · Yuki
  • Sees "You're live!" screen with confetti restraint (not tacky)
  • Receives credentials for SMBC Connect
  • Sees a walkthrough of what she can do next (move money, set up users, etc.)
  • Can download a summary pack (signed docs, config, product details)
RM · James
  • Hands off to service team via in-app workflow
  • Sets up ongoing relationship dashboard
  • Schedules 30/60/90 day check-ins (auto)
  • Application moves from "active onboarding" to "lifecycle monitoring"
AI · in background
  • Provisions accounts in core banking (mocked)
  • Starts perpetual KYC watchtower — triggers on registry changes, adverse media, sanctions updates
  • Schedules periodic review reminders based on risk tier
Teaser: what comes next

Stage 6 is also where we teaser Perpetual KYC — onboarding never really "ends" in modern banking. Every registry filing, every adverse media event, every periodic review triggers an update. We show this as a subtle "we're watching" card, hinting at a future prototype.

10 · Cross-cutting

The RM Copilot

Not a stage — a presence. Available anywhere in the RM cockpit via ⌘J or the prominent Fresh Green button. The fourth AI hero moment: natural language over the RM's entire portfolio.

◆ Hero Moment · RM Copilot

A colleague in a keystroke.

Persistent sidecar across /rm/*. Claude API backend, with tool-calling into Supabase so it can reason across applications, entities, documents, memos, and screening results for the RM's entire book.

Example prompts
  • "What's outstanding on Kaisei?"
  • "Draft a KYC memo summarising screening results"
  • "Compare Kaisei's risk profile against my portfolio average"
  • "Who else in my portfolio is expanding to Singapore? Any parallels?"
  • "Summarise this application in 3 bullets for my Monday call"
  • "What's my pipeline value at risk this week?"
